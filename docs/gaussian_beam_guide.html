1 · Gaussian Function

A Gaussian beam profile can be written in two equivalent and commonly used forms: one parameterised by the Full Width at Half Maximum (FWHM) and one by the 1/e² beam waist radius $w_0$.

Intensity profiles

(1a) $$I(x) = a\,e^{-4\ln 2\,(x-b)^2/\mathrm{FWHM}^2}$$
(1b) $$I(x) = a\,e^{-2(x-b)^2/w_0^2}$$

Where $a$ is the peak intensity and $b$ is the beam centre position.

Half-maximum conditions: $I(\mathrm{FWHM}/2) = a/2$ and $I(w_0) = a/e^2$.

FWHM ↔ 1/e² conversion

The FWHM diameter and the 1/e² radius $w_0$ are related by:

(2) $$\mathrm{FWHM} = w_0\sqrt{2\ln 2} \approx 1.177\,w_0$$

Equivalently: $w_0 = \mathrm{FWHM}/\sqrt{2\ln 2} \approx \mathrm{FWHM}/1.177$.

Rayleigh Range $z_R$

The Rayleigh range is the distance from the focal waist to where the beam radius grows by $\sqrt{2}$ (doubling the area and halving the fluence). $\lambda$ is the wavelength and $n$ is the refractive index.

(3) $$z_R = \frac{n\pi w_0^2}{\lambda}$$

M² (Beam Quality Factor)

To measure $M^2$, fit beam-width measurements $W(z)$ (half the D4σ diameter) to:

(4) $$W^2(z) = W_0^2 + M^4\!\left(\frac{\lambda}{\pi W_0}\right)^{\!2}(z - z_0)^2$$

For an ideal Gaussian beam $M^2 = 1$ and the D4σ radius equals the 1/e² radius.

2 · Power, Repetition Rate & Pulse Energy

For a pulsed laser, power, pulse energy, and repetition rate are simply related. A CW laser has only average power (no pulse energy concept applies).

$U$Pulse energyJ
$f$Repetition rate (frequency)Hz = 1/s
$P$Average powerW = J/s
(5) $$P = U \cdot f \qquad \Longleftrightarrow \qquad U = \frac{P}{f}$$

3 · Fluence & Intensity

SymbolQuantityUnit
$F$Fluence (energy per area)J/cm²
$F_0$Peak fluenceJ/cm²
$I_0$Peak intensityW/cm²
$t$Pulse duration (FWHM)s
$\phi$Angle of incidencerad

Average fluence

(6) $$F = \frac{U}{\pi w_{0x} w_{0y}} = \frac{2\ln 2\, U}{\pi\,\mathrm{FWHM}_x\,\mathrm{FWHM}_y}$$

Peak fluence

The on-axis (peak) fluence is twice the average fluence computed at the 1/e² level:

(7) $$F_0 = \frac{2U}{\pi w_{0x} w_{0y}} = \frac{4\ln 2\, U}{\pi\,\mathrm{FWHM}_x\,\mathrm{FWHM}_y}$$

Non-normal incidence

At angle $\phi$ from the surface normal, the fluence is reduced by $\cos\phi$:

(8) $$F_{\mathrm{angle}} = F_{\mathrm{normal}}\cos\phi$$

Peak power (Gaussian temporal pulse)

(9) $$P_0 = \sqrt{\frac{4\ln 2}{\pi}}\;\frac{U}{t} \approx \frac{0.94\,U}{t}$$

Peak intensity

Use the factor 0.94 for a Gaussian temporal profile; use 0.88 for a sech² profile.

(10) $$I_0 = \sqrt{\frac{4\ln 2}{\pi}}\;\frac{F_0}{t} \approx \frac{0.94\,F_0}{t}$$
Quick summary: Peak fluence = 2 × (energy / 1/e² beam area). Peak intensity = peak fluence / (effective pulse duration) × 0.94.
